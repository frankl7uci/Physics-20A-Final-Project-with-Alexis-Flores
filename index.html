Past Mars Rovers

Sojourner (1997)

Part of the Mars Pathfinder mission, Sojourner was the first rover to operate on Mars.

  • Size: 65 cm long
  • Weight: 11.5 kg
  • Mission: 83 days

Spirit (2004-2010)

Part of NASA's Mars Exploration Rover mission, Spirit operated until getting stuck in soft soil.

  • Size: 1.6 m long
  • Weight: 185 kg
  • Mission: 6 years, 2 months, 19 days

Opportunity (2004-2018)

NASA's record-breaking rover that operated for nearly 15 years before a dust storm ended its mission.

  • Size: 1.6 m long

The Future of Mars Exploration

With technology advancing more rapidly than ever and with the state of our planet deteriorating year by year, we think the future of mars exploration should focus on expanding the possibility of human life on another planet. Factors that are preventing mars from being habitable such as the strong storms, should be a priority in understanding. We have already seen examples of actions being taken to prepare human life on mars such as the efforts of spaceX and the MOXIE technology on the perseverance rover that produced oxygen on mars. Currently, spaceX is the closest to sending astronauts to mars with mission plans set for 2030. If successful, this will be a great breakthrough that will revolutionize the exploration of mars.
